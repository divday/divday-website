Keynote Speaker




Brandon “AK” Akpunku

Chief Excellence Orator at The One 3rd

Brandon “AK” Akpunku is the founder & host of The One 3rd, a podcast that allows diverse voices to share their experiences as business-minded creators, innovators, and entrepreneurs. Shining a light on the authentic journeys of challenges and triumphs, the podcast works to provide a platform that changes the narrative of diversity and inclusion one story at a time.

AK is a former pro athlete, that has transitioned into the tech ecosystem in Austin. By way of Dallas, his passion for challenging perspectives and seeking representation for those typically left out of the conversation has helped him build communities such as Young Black Austin.

Networking Session

Our networking session follows the style of a career fair. Company sponsors will set up tables, and you are free to walk around and talk to them about internship/full-time opportunities or any general questions. In addition, this is a great time to meet new people and build your network.

Diversity Panel

The Diversity Panel will have a moderator, and they will ask questions to our panelists about their struggles and experiences in the workforce. Afterwards, we will open up the floor for questions from the audience. Our panel will feature employes from Disney and Indeed, and Crystle Johnson, who currently works in Diversity, Inclusion, and CSR at EA!

Student Organizations




The Hispanic Association of Computer Scientists (HACS) is an organization that aims to empower and provide support for Hispanic students in computer science through academic, professional, and social events.

The Association of Black Computer Scientists (ABCS) is a student led organization that provides mentorship, networking, and opportunities to African and African-American students seeking an education in computer science.

About The Event

Div Day is a one day conference held in the GDC on February 15th from 9:00 AM to 4:00 PM to help empower historically marginalized communities interested in STEM. Div Day will have panels, a keynote speaker, and workshops to talk about topics such as managing a career shift and branding yourself. We aim to create an inclusive space to have conversations concerning prevalent issues in STEM for minority groups. There will be free food, swag, and companies like Disney, Google, and Indeed, making it a great networking opportunity. Check out our Facebook page and event for more information!
